**How I Organized OpenClaw + Telegram/Discord on a Dedicated Mac (100-Skill Playbook)**

## Post body (HTML-friendly plain text)

If you self-host an AI assistant like **OpenClaw** and connect **Telegram** or **Discord**, you quickly learn that your **daily Mac** and your **agent Mac** should not share the same risk profile. I’ve been collecting practices—pairing flows, allowlists, mention gates, gateway restarts—and wanted one place to browse them.

I’m sharing a **Next.js web app** called the **OpenClaw ↔ SNS Playbook**. It includes:

- **100 skills** grouped by lifecycle: Preplan, Plan, Execution, Post-plan, and Other (e.g. SMB file share between Macs when iCloud isn’t an option).
- **Per-channel prompts** for Telegram, Discord, Slack, Matrix, Signal, WhatsApp, iMessage, and a local web chat surface.
- For each **stage** and **audience level** (Basic / Intermediate / Advanced), **ten copy-pastable prompts** you can paste into your favorite LLM to go deeper.

**Why it matters:** Bot tokens and pairing codes belong on a machine you control and segment—not mixed with personal mail, photos, or a shared Apple ID.
